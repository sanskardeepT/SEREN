# SEREN — Business & Tone Guide

> **Source**: Condensed from SEREN Founding Team PDF.
> **Purpose**: Language rules, brand voice, and visual identity for all user-facing content.

---

## 1. Screening-Not-Diagnosis Language Rule

**SEREN is a screening tool, not a diagnostic tool.** This distinction must appear in every piece of user-facing copy, every report, and every description of what SEREN does.

### Required language patterns:
- ✅ "SEREN screens for…" / "SEREN identifies risk indicators for…"
- ✅ "This screening suggests…" / "Early signal detected for…"
- ✅ "We recommend consulting a professional for confirmation."
- ❌ Never use "diagnoses", "confirms", "certifies", or "clinically validates"

### Report language:
- Results are presented as **risk indicators**, not confirmations
- Every positive screening result includes a clear recommendation for professional referral
- The system is calibrated to prioritise **sensitivity** (catching genuine cases) over specificity
- A positive SEREN result makes professional referral "clearly and consistently warranted"

## 2. B2C Individual-App Positioning (Phase 2 — Current)

SEREN is currently being built as the **B2C individual app** — a direct-to-consumer product that any parent or adult can download and use independently, without needing a school involved.

### Positioning rules:
- Lead with the individual value: "screen yourself or your child"
- Emphasise no special equipment, no clinic visit, no expensive appointment
- Emphasise offline capability — works without internet
- Works on an ordinary phone
- Short daily sessions (10–15 minutes for intervention, few minutes for screening)

### Revenue model (B2C):
- Individual pays Rs. 200–300 per month for ongoing personalised treatment/practice
- This is 100% SEREN revenue (no school split)

### User flow:
1. **Screen** — Few minutes on simple, game-like tasks on phone
2. **Report** — Easy-to-understand, plain-language explanation (no medical jargon)
3. **Act** — Short daily practice routine built for that specific difficulty

## 3. Plain-Language Reporting Requirement

All SEREN reports must:
- Use **plain language** — no confusing medical jargon
- Explain exactly what area needs attention and why
- Be understandable by someone with no medical or psychological training
- Available in **Hindi, Marathi, and other Indian languages**
- Use **calm, non-alarming visual design** — the report should feel supportive, not scary
- Explicitly label confidence level based on how many modalities contributed (see training-protocols.md fusion table)

## 4. Brand Tone & Identity

### Tagline
> **"Every child is a star."**
> Extended: "Every child is a star. Every adult deserves a second chance."

### Emotional register:
- Warm, supportive, empowering — never clinical or cold
- Recognise the real human impact (reference the professor who still stammers, the child nobody noticed)
- Position SEREN as the thing that "could have changed the entire life path" of people with undetected conditions
- "We are not building 'another education app.'"

### Visual Identity

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Primary colour** | Dark Navy |
| **Accent colour 1** | Amber |
| **Accent colour 2** | Sage |
| **Typography (headings)** | Fraunces (if available as a font) |
| **Typography (body)** | Inter |
| **Motif** | Stars (aligned with "Every child is a star") |
| **Tone** | Premium, warm, trustworthy |

### Design principles:
- Dark navy base conveys trustworthiness and calm
- Amber provides warmth and highlights (CTAs, progress indicators, achievements)
- Sage adds softness and balance (success states, safe/calm zones)
- Star motif used throughout — progress indicators, achievement badges, branding elements
- Never use alarming colours (red warnings, harsh contrasts) in screening results
- All interactions should feel like encouragement, never punishment

## 5. Key Differentiators (for all copy)

When describing SEREN, always emphasise:
1. **One tool, 80+ conditions** — not just another single-purpose app
2. **Works on an ordinary phone** — no special equipment
3. **Works without internet** — built fully offline-first
4. **Government mandate alignment** — NEP 2020 requires this screening
5. **Screen → Report → Act** — doesn't just label, actually helps improve
6. **Children AND adults** — covers ages 5 through 60+

---

## 6. What SEREN Is NOT

Never position SEREN as:
- A replacement for professional clinical assessment
- A tool that requires trained clinicians to administer
- An education app (it's a screening + intervention platform)
- Only for children (adults are equally served)
- Only for schools (B2C individual use is the current phase)
